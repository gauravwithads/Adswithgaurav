Facebook Ads vs Instagram Ads: Which One Should Your Business Use?

Updated June 2026 · 6 min read

Both Facebook and Instagram ads run through the same Meta Ads Manager — which is exactly why so many Faridabad and NCR business owners get confused about which one to actually prioritise.

Here's a straightforward way to decide, based on what you're selling and who you're selling to.

The Quick Answer

If your business is visual (you can show a result, product, or space) and your audience is under 35, lean Instagram. If your audience is broader or older, or your offer is service-based and needs more explanation, lean Facebook. Most NCR businesses eventually use both — but starting with one focused budget is smarter than splitting thin from day one.

When Facebook Ads Make More Sense

  • Real estate in NCR — buyers often research carefully and respond well to detailed posts, photos and lead forms
  • Clinics and healthcare — an older patient demographic in Faridabad and NCR is often more active on Facebook
  • B2B or service businesses — where the buyer needs more context before reaching out

When Instagram Ads Make More Sense

  • Salons, spas, fitness studios — visually driven, younger audience actively looking for inspiration
  • Restaurants and cafes — food content performs exceptionally well as Reels
  • Fashion and retail — product showcase format suits Instagram's visual-first design
  • Coaching and education aimed at students/young professionals
One thing many business owners miss: when you run a campaign through Meta Ads Manager, you can usually choose to show it on Facebook, Instagram, or both — and let Meta's delivery system decide where your specific audience responds better. This is often smarter than manually picking one platform upfront.

A Simple Way to Decide for Your Business

  1. Look at your last 20 customers — are they mostly under 35 or a broader age range?
  2. Can you show your product/result visually in a photo or 15-second video?
  3. Does your offer need explanation, or does it sell itself visually?

If the answers point to "younger, visual, sells itself" — start with Instagram. If they point to "broader age range, needs explanation" — start with Facebook. Either way, track your cost-per-lead for the first 2–3 weeks before deciding to shift budget.

The Real Answer: Test Both, Briefly

If your budget allows it, the most reliable approach for NCR businesses is a short 2-week test split roughly evenly between both platforms, then shifting more budget toward whichever is converting at a lower cost-per-lead for your specific business — not based on general advice, but on your actual numbers.
